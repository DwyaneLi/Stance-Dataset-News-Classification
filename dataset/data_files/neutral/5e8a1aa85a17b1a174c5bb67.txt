The Government will be publishing a press release this Monday from the Ministry of Labour to ensure that workers in the public and private sector who will be quarantined due to the new coronavirus are "treated equally", said the Prime Minister this Monday.
"Treatment will be the same for all", said Antonio Costa in statements to journalists, after a visit to the Health and 24 contact centre in Lisbon, on the day when the first cases of infection in Portugal with the new coronavirus (Covid-19) were confirmed.
The head of the government stated that this Monday afternoon the Minister of Economic Affairs, Siza Vieira, is meeting with business associations and that the Minister of Labour "has a porter ready" and "awaits the end of this meeting" to publish it.
"What is guaranteed to all professionals, whether in the public sector or in the private sector, is that if necessary" a person "remains at home", will be "triggered a mechanism corresponding to the casualties, so that everyone can have their rights guaranteed", he said.
The Covid-19 outbreak, detected in December in China, which can cause respiratory infections such as pneumonia, has caused more than three.000 deaths and has infected almost 90 thousand people in 67 countries, including two in Portugal.
From infected people, about 45 thousand recovered.
In addition to the deaths of two.912 in China, there are reports of fatalities in Iran, Italy, South Korea, Japan, France, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Australia, Thailand, the United States of America and the Philippines.
A Portuguese crewman of a cruise ship is hospitalized in Japan for confirmation of infection.
The World Health Organisation (WHO) declared the Covid-19 outbreak an international public health emergency and increased the risk to "very high"Yeah.
In Portugal, the Directorate-General for Health (DGS) confirmed the first two cases of infection in Portugal, a 66-year-old man and a 33-year-old man, admitted to Porto hospitals.
