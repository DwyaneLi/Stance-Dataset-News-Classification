Vladivostok, February 6. /tass/ The coastal authorities are organizing an Expo to provide vegetables for coastal residents who are worried about a possible shortage due to pneumonia caused by the new coronavirus. Governor Oleg koremiako announced this in an interview with TASS.
"Because coastal people are worried about the possible shortage of vegetables, [Wednesday] at my direction Now the freight connection with China has been opened, and the products on the shelves should be enough. "There will be more vegetable fairs," said coermiako.
He added that if the price of vegetables in stores in the province is too high, residents in the area can turn to a hotline and the FBI will fix the problem. The governor noted that all necessary measures were being taken in the coastal areas to prevent the spread of coronavirus. As a result, three border cities have implemented a state of emergency system and no confirmed cases. In addition, authorities have helped the Russians return from China and have evacuated nearly 200 people.
The whole coastal area is one of the top 15 regions in Russia, and a rapid diagnosis and detection system of coronal disease has been obtained.
About Now, the health system has a clear virus detection algorithm. We are ready to deploy up to 1000 additional beds in our public health institutions when necessary. If necessary, facilities of the far east ministry of finance medical center and other federal and departmental medical institutions will be used. "
At the end of December 2019, Chinese authorities recorded an outbreak of pneumonia caused by 2019 ncov virus in Wuhan. In addition to China, 24 countries, including Russia, have found the disease. The World Health Organization recognizes an international emergency in Colorado, known as an epidemic with many hot spots. According to the latest data, there are more than 24300 cases of pneumonia caused by 2019 ncov virus in China. More than 900 people have been treated.
