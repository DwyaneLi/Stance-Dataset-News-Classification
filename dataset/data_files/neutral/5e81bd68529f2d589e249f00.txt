Moscow, 25 Russian news agency. Pavel kaczayev, vice chairman of the State Duma housing policy and residential utilities Committee, commented on RT's idea to suspend utility fees due to the situation in koronaverus.
He believes that it is unreasonable to exempt all Russians from paying utility fees due to the different levels of income of citizens. In addition, such measures may cause financial problems of enterprises.
Members also suggested that consideration be given to reducing the level of subsidies.
"Our average subsidy is that if you spend 22% of your money on public utilities, the state will subsidize this. Perhaps, he said, the board should be lowered: not 22%, but more than 10%.
Katchkayev warned that Moscow residents' public spending accounts for about 5% of household income. If the amount is large, the subsidy will be obtained from the capital government.
Previously, Vassily frasov, a member of the State Duma, proposed to exempt Russians from water and electricity charges during quarantine measures due to covid-19.
