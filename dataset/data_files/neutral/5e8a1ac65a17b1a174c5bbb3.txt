Angola has decreed mandatory quarantine, starting Tuesday, for all citizens who have been in China, South Korea, Iran and Italy, countries with "indigenous cases" of the Covid-19 outbreak, was announced today.
In a statement, the Angolan Ministry of Health explains that quarantine is mandatory for citizens who have travelled to Nigeria, Egypt and Algeria or who have had "contact with coronavirus-affected patients".
The quarantine shall be carried out for a minimum period of 14 days during which citizens are prevented from receiving any visitors.
The measure comes into force on Tuesday, March 03.
The Covid-19 epidemic caused by a new coronavirus, detected in China at the end of the year, has already infected 85.203 people, of whom 2.921 died, according to the authorities of the 60 affected countries and territories.
From infected people, over 36 thousand have recovered.
Only three cases of infection have been identified on the African continent, Egypt, Nigeria and Algeria.
